Problem Statement

Grand challenges in space synthetic biology

ABSTRACT

Space synthetic biology is a branch of biotechnology dedicated to engineering biological systems for space exploration, industry and science. There is significant public and private interest in designing robust and reliable organisms that can assist on long-duration astronaut missions. Recent work has also demonstrated that such synthetic biology is a feasible payload minimization and life support approach as well. This article identifies the challenges and opportunities that lie ahead in the field of space synthetic biology, while highlighting relevant progress. It also outlines anticipated broader benefits from this field, because space engineering advances will drive technological innovation on Earth

Resourse Utilization

The simple concept of resource utilization is:
Harvesting materials from the local environment on Mars instead of transporting them from Earth will save an enormous amount of money and reduce the weight of a launch vehicle drastically, making colonization possible.

Manufacturing

Manufacturing in space synthetic biology can leverage biological systems to process Martian regolith and produce silicon hydroxide or other valuable materials using biological pathways. These processes include using biological organisms like Diatoms(which incorporates silicon on its cell wall and deposits it to the regolith in the form of silica). And Silicon solubilizing bacteria which are capable of solubilizing silicon from silicon dioxide (Reaction which involve organic acids/compounds with SiO2 to give soluble Si compounds).

Space Medicine

To prevent microbial cell expiry, storage in a small lead-lined container while inactive is a feasible means of supplementing a microorganism's natural ability to withstand the harsh rigours of space, as when protected by rock. And because of the extremely small volume of a microbial colony, transporting the mass of such a shielding system is much more feasible than it would be for macroscopic organisms or abiotic systems.
